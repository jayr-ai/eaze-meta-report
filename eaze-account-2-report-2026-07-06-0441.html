Executive summary

Across the first two days of this week (Sun 5 Jul–Mon 6 Jul, the second day only partial), the account spent $799.61 across three active campaigns, producing 18 booked appointments and 6 website leads. All three campaigns spent this window — no legacy/residual campaigns logged any spend in this short window. Every table below reconciles to this $799.61 total except the platform, position, device and geography breakdowns, which were pulled a few minutes later mid-flight and are individually footnoted for the small amount of additional spend that landed in between.

  • Best appointment engine: Aashish Maru Medspa UGC Reels CBO at $26.42 per appointment on $237.80 spend — almost entirely carried by UGC Reel Ad 05 ($232.00 spend, 9 of the campaign's 9 appointments, $25.78 each).
  • Best lead engine: RPF 01 Dentists at $16.27 per lead on a 3.72% CTR and $97.60 spend — the same ad set Meta's opportunity score tool flags to scale, estimating 74% more conversions at higher budget.
  • Biggest drain: the Med Spa + Dental ABO Broad campaign, the largest spender at $464.21, at $51.58 per appointment — roughly double the UGC Reels campaign's cost per appointment. Within it, V1 Image 4 Med Spa is the weakest single ad: $131.44 for 1 appointment.
  • Notable change: opportunity score rose from 86 to 88 since last week's report — the two points are new: a "let Meta add music" recommendation (+2) that wasn't present last week, alongside the recurring reels/gen-AI/scale-campaign recommendations.

If the pixels are not matching, every cost per result above is unreliable. This account runs two pixels: the original "Eaze Consulting Pixel1" and a newer "Eaze Consulting" pixel. This is a current account-level health check, not specific to this reporting window, and scores are effectively unchanged from last week's report.

PixelLead matchSchedule matchSubmitAppVerdict
Eaze Consulting Pixel1 (original)9.29.18.2Strong
Eaze Consulting (newer)9.39.36.1Mostly strong

Lead and Schedule, the two events these campaigns optimize toward, score a strong 9.1–9.3 on both pixels with full email, phone and name coverage. SubmitApplication is uneven: 8.2 on the original pixel versus 6.1 on the newer pixel, which is missing email/phone/name match keys on that event. Nothing here undermines the cost-per-result numbers used elsewhere in this report, since every campaign optimizes toward Lead or Schedule, not SubmitApplication.

Separately — on the unreconciled breakdown tables above: the platform, platform position, device and geography breakdowns in sections 05, 06 and 08 each independently total roughly $798.1x, consistently about $1.48–$1.50 under the $799.61 account, campaign, ad, age, gender and dayparting totals (all of which reconcile exactly). This was investigated by re-running each of those four breakdowns twice — once with a higher row limit and reversed sort, and once again as part of the final consolidated batch used for this report — and the gap persisted at the same size both times, ruling out a row-limit or pagination cause. Age, gender and dayparting all capture the full account total, so this is not a case of Meta simply failing to return data for those extra dollars; it appears to be a small slice of delivery (roughly 0.2% of spend) that Meta's platform/position/device/region breakdown dimensions specifically do not attribute to any row, while its age/gender/hourly dimensions do. This is disclosed rather than forced to match, per this report's no-invented-numbers rule — flagging it to JV as worth a manual spot-check in Ads Manager if it recurs on a full 7-day pull.

  2. Keep UGC Reel Ad 05 running as-is. Its first two verified days this week (2.39%, 2.44% CTR) sit inside the same 2.39%–2.87% plateau band last week's 7-day report established — no sign of fatigue, no reason to refresh it yet.
