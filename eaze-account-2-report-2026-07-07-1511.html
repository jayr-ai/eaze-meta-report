Executive summary

Across the three settled days of this week (Sun Jul 5 – Tue Jul 7 — the advertiser's PST8PDT clock had not yet reached Wed Jul 8 at pull time), Eaze Account 2 spent $1,674.29 to generate 33 appointments and 10 leads. The RPF 01 dentist-leads campaign is the standout, converting at $18.35 per lead on a 4.29% CTR. Appointment volume was carried by two campaigns: AI Funding Solutions | Med Spa + Dental at $61.11 per appointment, and the Aashish Maru UGC Reels campaign at $31.90 per appointment — the latter is clearly the more efficient of the two, converting at nearly half the cost. Instagram absorbed 56% of spend but returned the account's weakest CTR (1.80%) and highest CPC ($8.89) of the two major platforms, while Facebook ran at less than half that CPC. The opportunity score held at 84/100, unchanged from the prior pull roughly 30 minutes earlier, with no structural account changes in between.

  • Best appointment engine: Aashish Maru [Do Not Touch] | Medspa UGC Reels — $31.90 per appointment, 18 appointments, 2.02% CTR.
  • Best lead engine: RPF 01 - Dentists - WV - Images — $18.35 per lead, 10 leads, 4.29% CTR (the account's only lead-generating campaign this window).
  • Biggest drain: Instagram as a platform — $942.54 (56% of spend) at $8.89 CPC and 1.80% CTR, the account's least efficient major channel.
  • Notable change: Opportunity score unchanged at 84/100 vs. the prior tracked pull ~30 minutes earlier; spend rose $15.99 with no new conversions recorded yet, consistent with attribution lag.

Data & actions12

Action list

  1. Scale RPF 01 - Dentists - WV - Images. $18.35 per lead on a 4.29% CTR is the account's most efficient engine by a wide margin, and Meta's own opportunity score flags it as stable enough to scale (+74% conversions estimated).
  2. Address the Instagram CPC gap. Instagram takes 56% of spend ($942.54) at $8.89 CPC vs. Facebook's $3.69 — more than double. Test shifting budget weight toward Facebook placements or letting Advantage+ placements rebalance automatically.
  3. Prep a refresh for UGC Reel Ad 05. CTR fell every day this window (2.51% → 1.83% → 1.55%), a real decline, not a two-point illusion — it's still the top spender at $543.36, so fatigue here has outsized budget impact.
  4. Reduce or exclude the 10 PM hour. 22:00-22:59 is the worst dayparting slot on both CTR (0.96%) and CPC ($14.30) — a clear candidate for a bid reduction or scheduling exclusion.
  5. Lean into the noon hour. 12:00-12:59 is the best-value hour on both CTR (5.14%) and CPC ($2.71) at meaningful spend ($97.44) — worth testing a bid boost or added budget in that window.
  6. Review Arizona targeting. The only top-10 state that's worst on both CTR (2.00%) and CPC ($9.60) — worth narrowing the audience there or excluding until efficiency improves.
  7. Apply the A+ Creative enhancements bundle. The single highest-value item in Meta's opportunity score (+4 points, ~3% lower cost/result) across 10 already-running ads — the cheapest lift available this run.
  8. Verify pixel field capture on the "Eaze Consulting" pixel. Its Lead event match quality (7.4) trails "Eaze Consulting Pixel1" (8.9) because email/phone/name are only captured on 38.5% of events — tightening form-field requirements on whichever landing page feeds that pixel would firm up cost-per-lead confidence account-wide.

Overall direction: the three live campaigns are healthy and tracking consistently with the prior pull — spend is accruing steadily with no drop in conversion pace, RPF 01 remains an under-scaled efficiency lever, and the account's biggest structural opportunity is fixing the Instagram/Facebook cost imbalance.
