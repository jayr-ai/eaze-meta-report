Executive summary

Across the window (3 full settled days, Sun Jul 5 – Tue Jul 7, plus roughly 8 hours of Wed Jul 8 on the advertiser's PST8PDT clock at pull time), Eaze Account 2 spent $2,290.35 to generate 41 appointments and 12 leads. The Aashish Maru UGC Reels campaign is the most efficient appointment engine at $39.88 per appointment, well ahead of the broader Med Spa + Dental ABO campaign at $59.05 per appointment. RPF 01 remains the account's only lead-generating campaign, converting at $17.86 per lead on a 4.52% CTR — still the single cheapest result type in the account. Instagram again absorbed the majority of spend (57.0%, $1,307.09) at the account's weakest CTR (2.02%) and highest CPC ($8.33) among the major platforms, while Facebook ran at less than half that CPC. The opportunity score slipped from 84 to 82, the first drop across tracked pulls, driven mainly by a newly-surfaced 7-point A+ Creative enhancements bundle Meta has not yet had applied.

  • Best appointment engine: Aashish Maru [Do Not Touch] | Medspa UGC Reels — $39.88 per appointment, 18 appointments, 2.00% CTR.
  • Best lead engine: RPF 01 - Dentists - WV - Images — $17.86 per lead, 12 leads, 4.52% CTR (the account's only lead-generating campaign this window).
  • Biggest drain: Instagram as a platform — $1,307.09 (57.0% of spend) at $8.33 CPC and 2.02% CTR, the account's least efficient major channel.
  • Notable change: Opportunity score fell from 84 to 82 since the last report; the "Eaze Consulting" pixel's Lead match quality also regressed from 7.4 to 6.1 with zero PII keys now captured — both flagged for review in Sections 10–11.

Action list

  1. Apply the A+ Creative enhancements bundle. Now the single highest-value item in Meta's opportunity score (+7 points, 3% lower cost/result) across 7 already-running ads — the cheapest lift available this run, and the main driver behind the score's 2-point drop while it sits unapplied.
  2. Scale RPF 01 - Dentists - WV - Images. $17.86 per lead on a 4.52% CTR is the account's most efficient engine by a wide margin, and Meta's own opportunity score flags it as stable enough to scale (+74% conversions estimated).
  3. Investigate the "Eaze Consulting" pixel's Lead match regression. Lead event match quality fell from 7.4 to 6.1 since the last report, with zero PII match keys (email/phone/name) now captured versus partial coverage before — check whether a form-field change or tracking break hit whichever landing page feeds this pixel.
  4. Prep a refresh for "V1 | Image 4 | Med Spa." CTR fell on every full day this window (2.69% → 1.68% → 1.49%, continuing to 0.84% in the partial fourth day) — the clearest fatigue signal in the account, on an ad still spending $365.23.
  5. Address the Instagram CPC gap. Instagram takes 57.0% of spend ($1,307.09) at $8.33 CPC vs. Facebook's $3.69 — more than double. Test shifting budget weight toward Facebook placements or letting Advantage+ placements rebalance automatically.
  6. Reduce or exclude the 2 PM hour. 14:00-14:59 is the worst dayparting slot on both CTR (1.57%) and CPC ($9.73) — a clear candidate for a bid reduction or scheduling exclusion.
  7. Lean into the noon hour. 12:00-12:59 is the best-value hour on both CTR (5.12%) and CPC ($2.71) at meaningful spend ($97.55) — worth testing a bid boost or added budget in that window.
  8. Watch "V1 | AI Image 2 | Dentist" for a confirmed recovery. CTR has climbed for two straight readings (1.89% → 2.51% → 3.65%), but the latest point is a partial, early-hours day — confirm the trend holds once Jul 8 fully settles before reallocating budget toward it.

Overall direction: the three live campaigns are healthy and growing steadily on volume — spend, appointments, and leads are all up since the last report — but the opportunity score's first-ever dip and a real pixel match-quality regression mean this is a run to act on the Data trust and Opportunities sections, not just the media performance numbers.
