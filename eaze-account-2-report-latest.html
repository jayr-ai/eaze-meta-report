Overview00

Executive summary

Across the settled portion of the week (Sun–Mon, Jul 5–6), Eaze Account 2 spent $1,092.62 across three active campaigns, producing 23 med spa appointments at a blended $41.71 each and 8 dental leads at $16.66 each — both blended figures computed by dividing total spend for each result type by its result count, carried to four decimal places before rounding. Instagram absorbed 56% of spend ($612.78 of $1,092.62) at 1.99% CTR, versus Facebook's 3.49% CTR on $448.50 — nearly double the engagement rate for less than half the budget. The iPhone/Instagram combination continues to carry the account's spend at a real efficiency discount versus Android and Facebook. Both flagship appointment ads (UGC Reel Ad 05 and V1 | AI Image 2 | Dentist) again posted a lower CTR on their second day than their first, but with still only two days of data on record this remains too early to call fatigue rather than normal week-open variance.

  • Best appointment engine: Aashish Maru UGC Reels (CBO, Demographic) at $32.17 per appointment, 11 appointments — 36% cheaper per result than the ABO Med Spa + Dental campaign ($50.45/appointment).
  • Best lead engine: RPF 01 - Dentists at $16.66 per lead, 8 leads — the account's only lead-generating campaign this window.
  • Biggest drain: five near-zero orphan ads (UGC Reel Ad 03, V1 | AI Image 1 | Dentist, V1 | Image 1 | Med Spa, V1 | AI Image 2 | Med Spa, UGC Reel Ad 04) — $16.40 combined spend, zero results across both days. This is the same group and the same figure used again in section 03 and section 12 below.
  • Notable change: spend continued climbing on the same Sun–Mon window (+$29.17 vs the last run) with no new appointments or leads yet recorded; the opportunity score held flat at 81/100.

Data & actions12

Action list

  1. Apply Meta's A+ Creative enhancements bundle across the account. It's the single highest-leverage recommendation available (7-point opportunity score lift, 3% lower cost per result) and already covers nearly every active ad.
  2. Let Meta auto-add music to the 3 eligible ads for a further 2-point score lift and ~5% lower cost per result, at no creative cost.
  3. Investigate why the score sits at 81, not 88. The drop happened earlier on Monday and has held flat across two consecutive pulls 45 minutes apart — confirm in Ads Manager whether the A+ Creative bundle is a genuinely new opportunity or a scoring recalculation, since the two call for different responses.
  4. Hold off on judging creative fatigue. UGC Reel Ad 05 (2.51%→1.59%), V1 | AI Image 2 | Dentist (2.93%→2.21%), and V1 | Image 4 | Med Spa (2.70%→2.03%) all softened day-over-day, but with only 2 days of data this could be normal week-open pacing. Re-check once a full 7-day window is on record.
  5. Rebalance platform delivery away from Instagram. Facebook runs 3.49% CTR vs Instagram's 1.99% CTR, yet Instagram holds 56% of spend ($612.78 vs $448.50).
  6. Shift budget toward Android where efficiency allows. Android smartphone runs 3.93% CTR at $2.80 CPC vs iPhone's 2.44% CTR at $6.16 CPC, but iPhone still carries 77% of spend.
  7. Retire or refresh the five zero-result orphan ads (UGC Reel Ad 03, V1 | AI Image 1 | Dentist, V1 | Image 1 | Med Spa, V1 | AI Image 2 | Med Spa, UGC Reel Ad 04) — $16.40 combined spend with no results across two days.
  8. Scale the Aashish Maru UGC Reels campaign cautiously; at $32.17/appointment it is 36% cheaper than the ABO Med Spa + Dental campaign's $50.45/appointment, and Meta's own opportunity score data flags a related ad set as a scale candidate (74% more conversions estimated).

Two days into the week, the account remains fundamentally healthy — spend that reconciles to the account total across every table in this report, and two distinct efficient engines (UGC Reels for appointments, RPF 01 for leads) — with Instagram/iPhone delivery efficiency and the flagship ads' early CTR dip the two things worth watching as the rest of the week fills in.
